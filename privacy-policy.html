Privacy Policy for Lenny Kivuti Bus Party Ambassadors

Effective Date: February 7, 2026 | Aligned with Youth Movement Framework

Welcome to the Lenny Kivuti Bus Party Ambassadors ("we", "our", "us", "the Movement"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information in accordance with our Youth Movement Framework Document. Our services include the website at Bus Party Youth Platform , recruitment activities, events, and all related youth programs. empowerment initiatives (collectively, the "Service").

By registering as a member, participating in our activities, or accessing the Service, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy and acknowledge that your data will be handled as outlined in our BUS AMBASSADORS FRAMEWORK.

1. Information We Collect

a. Personal Information for Membership Registration

As outlined in our Framework (Section 2.2), we collect personal information when you:

  • Register for membership (Youth Members aged 18-35 or Associate Members)
  • Sign up for events, workshops, or training programs
  • Apply for leadership positions within the movement
  • Participate in recruitment drives at ward, constituency, or institutional levels
  • Contact us with inquiries or feedback

This includes mandatory information as per Framework Section 1.1-1.3:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Date of birth (to verify youth membership eligibility: 18-35 years)
  • National ID number (for voter registration and verification purposes)
  • Residential information (Embu County residency verification)
  • Educational institution (for student representatives and institutional engagement)
  • T-shirt size (for official movement apparel as per Code of Conduct 2.2)
  • Membership category (Youth Member or Associate Member)
  • Unique Membership ID (issued upon registration)

b. Special Category Data

In alignment with our commitment to inclusive leadership (Framework Section 2.4), we may collect:

  • Information related to Special Interest Groups (Youths, People Living with Disabilities, Women)
  • Data for ensuring diversity in leadership representation

c. Automatically Collected Information

When you interact with our digital platforms (as described in Framework Section 3.2), we collect:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited on our website and member portal
  • Interaction with our social media campaigns
  • Registration and activity timestamps

d. Financial Information

In accordance with Framework Section 5 (Financial Regulations):

  • Membership dues payment information
  • Donation records
  • Fundraising participation data
  • Financial transaction records (maintained by Treasurer)

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for purposes outlined in our Framework Objectives (Section 2):

  • Membership Management: Maintaining the membership register (Framework 2.3)
  • Leadership Development: Identifying and nurturing young leaders (Objective 2)
  • Event Coordination: Organizing ward-level activities, training, and community events
  • Voter Mobilization: Sensitizing youth for voter registration and participation (Objective 8)
  • Transparency & Accountability: Financial reporting and activity monitoring (Objective 10)
  • Capacity Building: Providing targeted training based on member interests
  • Communication: Sending updates about meetings, events, and movement activities
  • Research: Conducting surveys for policy influence (Objective 11)
Important: We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties. All data handling aligns with our Framework's commitment to transparency and accountability.

3. How We Share Information

Information may be shared as follows, in accordance with our Framework:

  • Within Movement Leadership: With committee members for operational purposes
  • Ward/Constituency Levels: With Ward Managers and Constituency Managers for local engagement
  • Event Partners: With community organizations for joint activities (Objective 9)
  • Party Structure: With Bus Party officials for alignment and coordination
  • Legal Requirements: If required by law or to protect rights and safety
  • Audit Purposes: With external auditors for financial review (Framework Section 5.1)

Associate Members' information is handled separately as per Framework Section 1.2, with distinct rights and participation levels.

4. Data Retention and Storage

We retain personal information in accordance with Framework requirements:

  • Membership records are maintained for active participation tracking
  • Financial records are kept for 7 years for audit purposes (Framework Section 5)
  • Leadership election records are archived for transparency
  • Inactive members' data may be archived after 2 years of inactivity

5. Security of Your Information

We implement security measures aligned with our Framework's commitment to responsible stewardship:

  • Financial data is protected with joint signatory requirements (Framework 7.1)
  • Member databases are accessible only to authorized committee members
  • Regular financial audits ensure data integrity (Framework 5.1)
  • Physical records are stored securely at designated movement offices

6. Your Rights as a Member

Based on Framework Section 3 (Rights of Members):

  • Youth Members (18-35): Access to information, voting rights, leadership candidacy
  • Associate Members: Participation in activities (excluding elections), access to information
  • All Members: Right to feedback, participation in capacity building, expression of concerns
  • Data Rights: Request correction of inaccurate information, opt-out of certain communications

7. Special Provisions for Youth Members

Given our focus on youth empowerment (Framework Section 1.1):

  • Special protections for members under 18 where applicable
  • Age verification processes for leadership eligibility
  • Targeted communication for different youth demographics
  • Parental consent considerations for minor participants in specific activities

8. Code of Conduct Compliance

Data handling complies with our Code of Conduct (Framework Section 4):

  • No discriminatory use of member information
  • Respect for member privacy in all communications
  • Proper use of official T-shirt distribution records
  • Ethical handling of disciplinary records

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to align with Framework revisions. Changes will be communicated through:

  • Movement conventions and general meetings
  • Official communication channels (WhatsApp groups, email newsletters)
  • Updates on our website and member portal

10. Contact and Reporting

For privacy concerns or data-related inquiries, contact the appropriate committee members as per Framework:
